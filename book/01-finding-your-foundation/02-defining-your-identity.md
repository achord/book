# Defining Your Identity

- [ ] Read book, man's search for meaning


A man must decide, consciously, what kind of person he want to become. He must intentionaly define
- what he stands for
- What his principles are
- How he wants to be seen
- How he identifies.

How a man identifies will directly influence his actions, which directly influence and predict with his success or failures.

E.g. (ChatGpt)
Identity as a Responsible Leader
A man defines himself as a responsible leader who values integrity, accountability, and effective communication. With this identity in mind, he focuses on leading by example, treating his team with respect, making informed decisions, and taking ownership of mistakes. Over time, his consistent leadership behavior earns him trust and respect, leading to successful career advancement and the creation of a cohesive, productive team.


This is easier said than done. Especially if you're made it three or more decades and have never asked yourself this question. Now you might need to undo bad habits and small thinking. Nut understand that with determination an humility, a person can invent themselves.  You can become the kind of person that want to be. What no one tells you is that it can be a hard path, but it is surely a path worth taking.

## Exercise

To turn this into an exercise, we can break it down into a series of reflective prompts that guide someone toward writing a small paragraph defining themselves. Here's a step-by-step framework for the exercise:

### **Exercise: Defining Your Identity**

Get out a pen and paper, or laptop, etc.

**Step 1: Values & Principles**
- **Prompt**: What are the core values and principles that guide your actions and decisions?
  *Example*: "I value integrity, creativity, and continuous growth."

**Step 2: Strengths & Passions**
- **Prompt**: What are your strengths and passions? What do you enjoy doing, and where do you excel?
  *Example*: "I am passionate about building things, whether it's software, art, or a business."

**Step 3: Vision for Yourself**
- **Prompt**: How do you want to be seen by others? What identity do you want to project?
  *Example*: "I want to be seen as a responsible leader who is also compassionate and innovative."

**Step 4: Aspirations**
- **Prompt**: Who do you aspire to be? What kind of person do you want to become in the future?
  *Example*: "I aspire to be someone who creates meaningful work while also contributing positively to the community."

**Step 5: Writing the Paragraph**
- Combine your responses into a single paragraph that defines your identity.
  *Example*:
  "I am a creative and responsible leader, driven by a passion for building innovative solutions. I value integrity, growth, and compassion, striving to be someone who contributes positively to both my work and community. My goal is to continuously evolve, lead with empathy, and stay committed to creating meaningful, impactful work."

### **Exercise: Defining Your Identity**

**Step 1: Values & Principles**
- **Prompt**: What are the core values and principles that guide your actions and decisions?
  *Example*: I value integrity, creativity, continuous growth, and empathy towards others. I define success by living up to ones potential, and it is my life's mission to live up to mine.

**Step 2: Strengths & Passions**
- **Prompt**: What are your strengths and passions? What do you enjoy doing, and where do you excel?
  *Example*:
  - I am passionate about building things, whether it's software, art, or music
  - Empathy in problem solving
  - Creativity
  - Tenacity
  - Spotting talent and quality in art, music, etc.


**Step 3: Vision for Yourself**
- **Prompt**: How do you want to be seen by others? What identity do you want to project?
  *Example*:
I want to be seen as
- builder of important things.
- someone who is doing work in his own lane, following his passion, and excelling at what I do.
- someone who inspires others to follow their passion.
- A creative leader in design and music.
- Someone who does the impossible.
- Someone who produces work with a wow factor and mystery and make people ask, How does he do that?



**Step 4: Aspirations**
- **Prompt**: Who do you aspire to be? What kind of person do you want to become in the future?
  *Example*:
  I aspire to be the kind of person who:
- Creates meaningful work
- Provides adventure for my family
- Provides stability for my family
- Can take on large tasks
- Has a body of great work

**Step 5: Writing the Paragraph**
- Combine your responses into a single paragraph that defines your identity.
  *Example*:
  (Needs work)
I am someone who values integrity, creativity, continuous growth, and empathy, striving to live up to my potential and help others do the same. I am passionate about building things, whether in software, art, or music, and I excel at problem-solving with empathy, creativity, and tenacity. I am someone who can spot talent and quality in everything from art to music. I want to be seen as a builder of important things, someone who works in his own lane, following his passion and inspiring others to do the same. I aspire to be a creative leader in design and music, producing work that surprises people and makes them wonder how it was done. My goal is to create meaningful work, take on large tasks, and provide both stability and adventure for my family, building a body of work that truly matters
